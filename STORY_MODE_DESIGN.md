# Story Mode Design

## Goals

- Build a full story mode made of 20+ campaigns.
- Give every campaign its own sub-story, escalation, setback, and payoff.
- Keep mission flow varied so the player is not asked to repeat the same verb loop for hours.
- Use the current sandbox strengths of Sindicate: driving, collisions, wanted pressure, pickups, service vehicles, wreck recovery, and city traversal.
- Take inspiration from GTA 2 mission structure and pacing without borrowing its factions, names, or plot.

## Story Premise

Sindicate story mode follows Rook Vance, a courier and wheelman who comes back to the city after their sister Nia, a municipal dispatcher, disappears. Nia left behind fragments of a hidden routing system called the Switchboard, a private network used by criminal crews, corrupt officials, and logistics companies to steer police response, ambulance coverage, tow recovery, and street congestion for profit.

At first, Rook is only trying to find Nia. That search pulls them into a city-wide shadow war over who controls movement itself: who gets delayed, who gets rescued, which wrecks vanish, which districts burn, and who profits when panic hits the streets. By the end of the story, Rook is not just exposing one murderer or one gang boss. They are dismantling an entire machine that turns the city into a market of engineered emergencies.

## Core Story Pillars

- The city is the weapon. Roads, bridges, garages, hospitals, tow yards, taxi routes, and police patrol lanes matter to the plot.
- Every chapter changes who is using the city better: gangs, corporations, public services, or the player.
- Missions escalate from local hustles to district-level sabotage to citywide control failure.
- The player should regularly feel like they are learning a new use for familiar systems, not replaying the same job with bigger numbers.

## Chapter Structure

This story is split into 24 chapters. Each chapter is intended to become one future campaign with 5 missions.

Recommended rhythm inside every campaign:

1. Entry mission that teaches the chapter problem.
2. Complication mission that adds pressure or time risk.
3. System mission that uses a different verb or vehicle.
4. Reversal mission where the enemy answers back.
5. Payoff mission that resolves the chapter and points into the next one.

## Act I - Find The Missing Dispatcher

### Chapter 1 - Dead Drop District

Story role: Rook returns to the waterfront, learns Nia was moving evidence, and discovers someone is already cleaning up her trail.

Mission shape:

1. Night Ferry Run: Drive from the bus depot to the old dock motel while avoiding escalating patrol attention. This is a soft onboarding mission focused on route choice, near misses, and learning that the city is hostile before the first gunfire starts.
2. Burned Locker: Reach three storage lockers before cleanup crews do, grab ledger fragments from each, and escape after the last pickup triggers a wanted spike. The goal is fast traversal with a changing risk profile instead of simple collection.
3. Wreck Before Dawn: Cause a controlled crash to block a cleanup van, then fight or evade the crew long enough to steal its cargo manifest. This introduces deliberate collision as a mission tool.
4. False Ambulance: Chase a fake ambulance that collected one of Nia's contacts and stop it anywhere on the route before it reaches the chop garage. Boxing it in or blowing it up should both count, so the mission plays like an interception instead of a forced tail sequence.
5. Last Call At Pier 9: Assault the pier office, eliminate marked cleaners, retrieve the dispatch badge inside, and survive the counterpush until the road out reopens. This closes the chapter with a compact siege.

### Chapter 2 - Spare Parts Gospel

Story role: The trail points toward independent tow operators who know where the city hides inconvenient wrecks and bodies.

Mission shape:

1. Yard Talk: Steal a tow truck, complete one legitimate recovery to earn the crew's trust, then return to the yard before the truck is flagged stolen. This uses the service loop as social infiltration.
2. Hook Chain: Recover two specific wrecks before a rival yard gets them first, with each wreck spawning different road hazards and ambush angles. This adds race pressure to a service mission.
3. The Empty Shell: Escort a stripped sedan carrying hidden documents across hostile streets while the car becomes harder to protect each time it collides. The tension comes from preserving fragile cargo, not kill count.
4. Crusher Feed: Infiltrate a scrap plant, trigger the machinery, and escape while enemy drivers try to ram you back into the yard. This is a moving environmental hazard mission.
5. Towline Oath: Defend the yard during a night raid, keep three garage bays usable, and chase the raiders to their dispatcher contact. This makes the player defend locations instead of only attacking them.

### Chapter 3 - Static On The Hospital Band

Story role: Nia's final calls mention missing ambulance routes and patients who never reached intake.

Mission shape:

1. Cold Intake: Steal ambulance access, pick up an injured witness before a rival squad reaches the body, and deliver them alive to a safe clinic. This is a timed rescue instead of a corpse recovery loop.
2. Flatline Gap: Visit four dead radio zones and plant relay beacons while the district gradually fills with enemy cars drawn by the signal. This is a multi-stop setup mission with growing heat.
3. Clean Sheets: Sneak into a hospital loading tunnel in a service vehicle, avoid raising a full alarm, and photograph the falsified transfer records. This is a low-combat infiltration mission.
4. Crash Cart: After the witness is found, race a damaged ambulance through blocked intersections while preserving enough vehicle health to keep the patient alive. This creates urgency through vehicle condition.
5. Ward 6 Exit: Escort a nurse hacker out of the hospital district during an active lockdown where roads close one by one. The mission is about rerouting under dynamic constraints.

### Chapter 4 - Meter Running

Story role: Taxi dispatch logs show Nia was using civilian rides to move informants under the radar.

Mission shape:

1. Ghost Fare: Pick up a passenger who refuses to reveal their identity, follow a sequence of strange drop-off instructions, and realize you are being tested. The mission is narrative-heavy and uses taxi play for suspense.
2. Double Booking: Complete two overlapping fares, choosing which passenger to delay, with each decision changing the ambush at the end. This introduces small branching inside one mission.
3. Red Light Choir: Use a cab to tail a radio host through nightlife traffic without spooking their bodyguards, then steal the tape from their producer's car. This is a stealth chase in plain sight.
4. Meter Burn: Run contraband through police checkpoints disguised as a normal fare, where sharp driving lowers suspicion and reckless driving spikes it. This makes clean driving strategically useful.
5. Farewell Signal: Pick up a dying dispatcher who gives Rook Nia's next clue while assassins try to box the cab into dead ends. The chapter ends with a protective escape instead of a fight-to-clear.

### Chapter 5 - Precinct Ashes

Story role: Rook learns corrupt police are renting response delays to the highest bidder.

Mission shape:

1. Badge Borrower: Steal a patrol car, use it to reach sealed streets, and scan three evidence caches before the plate gets reported. This is access-based infiltration through disguise.
2. Suspect Carousel: Frame a gang lieutenant by planting evidence in their convoy, then survive the chaos when the police crackdown expands faster than planned. This mission is about manipulating wanted systems.
3. Lockup Blackout: Cut power to a precinct annex, enter during the confusion, and free a prisoner who knows where Nia was headed. This combines sabotage, reach, and escort.
4. Riot Route: Hold a district corridor open for escaping civilians while both police and gang vehicles try to reclaim it. This is area denial through movement.
5. Hard Copy: Hit the records room, destroy digital backups, steal the paper ledger, and escape a precinct-wide dragnet. This is a layered raid mission that closes Act I.

### Chapter 6 - The Switchboard Name

Story role: The paper ledger finally reveals the hidden network's name and shows that multiple power blocs are feeding it.

Mission shape:

1. Dead Letter Branch: Reach a shuttered post office that once handled dispatch reroutes, open hidden lockers in sequence, and escape before the building is burned. This is a navigation puzzle with a hard clock.
2. Relay Theft: Hijack a van carrying switch hardware, then choose a safehouse route while every faction wants the cargo intact. The pressure is from pursuit density, not just one boss car.
3. Blue Map Room: Intercept a planner moving street-closure blueprints, capture them alive, and keep them alive until questioning is done. This is a live-target control mission.
4. Four Minute Silence: Survive a blackout district where traffic lights, emergency routes, and radio guidance all fail at once. The mission sells what citywide failure feels like.
5. Name In The Static: Drive to a rooftop transmitter, upload the stolen hardware, and defend the site long enough to decrypt the first full Switchboard file. Act I ends with proof that the conspiracy is bigger than one department.

## Act II - Court The City's Middle Powers

### Chapter 7 - Freight Union Morning

Story role: Rook approaches the dock freight union, which hates the Switchboard because rerouted inspections are crushing independent shipping.

Mission shape:

1. Union Test Run: Move three cargo trailers across the harbor without losing them to sabotage. This is a slow, deliberate logistics mission rather than a speed test.
2. Picket Line Breaker: Clear hostile blockers from a strike route while protecting non-combatant workers on foot. The tension comes from selective aggression.
3. Harbor Echo: Board a moving ferry convoy, fight across vehicle tops and deck lanes, and seize a crate of falsified customs tags. This is a set-piece mission built around unusual terrain.
4. Crane Jam: Trigger two dock cranes to trap an enemy convoy, then escape the collapsing yard perimeter. This is a trap mission built from environment interaction.
5. The Long Manifest: Escort the union leader through a rolling ambush while documents are broadcast citywide. This chapter secures Rook's first real ally bloc.

### Chapter 8 - Neon Couriers

Story role: Street racers and courier crews know how to move through the city faster than official systems do.

Mission shape:

1. Signal Sprint: Beat a courier team across multiple route options, with shortcuts hidden through alleys and service lanes. This is a race mission where map knowledge matters more than raw top speed.
2. Drop Stack: Make five timed dead drops in one run, but each delivered package alters the police pattern for the next stop. The route evolves as the mission progresses.
3. Blind Corner: Smuggle a passenger who cannot be seen by cameras, forcing the player to choose low-surveillance roads over the shortest roads. This turns route identity into a mechanic.
4. Rival Tape: Ram a data bike crew off their line, recover their cassette, and reach a decoder before backup arrives. This is a compact attack-and-extract mission.
5. Lamps Out: Shut down a boulevard festival by hitting power vans in the correct order so the courier network can move unseen. The order creates tactical variety over simple destruction.

### Chapter 9 - Glass Towers, Empty Floors

Story role: Corporate property managers are using staged accidents to depress district prices before buying them up.

Mission shape:

1. Tenant Warning: Deliver eviction evidence to three tenant leaders while private security cars try to box you away from each building entrance. This is a delivery mission under harassment.
2. Window Tax: Plant charges on luxury generator nodes, but only after stealing the matching maintenance keys from different vehicles. This spaces setup and payoff across the map.
3. Lobby Flood: Trigger a sprinkler panic in one tower to draw out a broker in another, then tail them to an underground garage meeting. This is a misdirection mission.
4. Fire Sale Run: Steal a box truck filled with forged deeds and keep it under a damage threshold until it reaches the press. This is defensive driving under pressure.
5. Vacancy Notice: Storm a half-built tower at dusk, fight upward floor by floor, and seize the transaction archive from the penthouse shell. This chapter ends on vertical escalation.

### Chapter 10 - Saints Of The Side Street

Story role: A neighborhood aid network has been illegally covering for residents abandoned by official services.

Mission shape:

1. Soup Line Watch: Protect a mobile kitchen route from extortion crews without destroying the van carrying the supplies. This is crowd-defense through restraint.
2. Siren Swap: Trade a marked ambulance for an unmarked clinic van in motion, then carry medicine through a police sweep. This is a precision handoff mission.
3. Half Block Safehouse: Gather scattered residents and lead them to shelter while roads are collapsing behind you. The mission uses sequential rescue instead of one VIP escort.
4. Medicine Debt: Collect stolen pharmaceuticals from black-market sellers, where some surrender peacefully and others turn the exchange into a trap. This breaks up combat tone.
5. Quiet Chapel: Hold a church clinic during a coordinated raid and evacuate the doctors through the cemetery gate after the front entrance fails. This provides a strong defensive chapter climax.

### Chapter 11 - Broadcast Teeth

Story role: A pirate radio network can expose Switchboard operations, but half its hosts are compromised.

Mission shape:

1. Antenna Climb: Reach three hilltop repeaters under sniper and vehicle pressure to restore the network. This is a positional control mission.
2. Open Mic Trap: Deliver bait intel on air, then reach the ambush site first and shape the battlefield before the target arrives. This is a preparation mission.
3. Jingle Bomb: Chase a propaganda van across commercial streets and disable it without destroying the audio master inside. This rewards careful takedown.
4. Studio Sweep: Enter a live station mid-broadcast, identify the mole among multiple staffers, and capture them before they erase the backup tape. This is a fast read-and-react mission.
5. Citywide Readout: Defend the rooftop transmitter while stolen evidence plays across the city and every hostile faction converges. This chapter raises open war stakes.

### Chapter 12 - Debt Collection Weather

Story role: Loan sharks tied to the Switchboard are using fake service callouts to trap whole blocks in unpayable damage cycles.

Mission shape:

1. Missed Payment: Rescue a shop owner before enforcers drag them into a van. This is a short interception mission.
2. Three Stores Down: Visit multiple extorted businesses, collect testimony, and keep each witness alive until the final rendezvous. This is a distributed protection mission.
3. Ledger Heat: Steal the collector's car, then drive through marked checkpoints to auto-copy the encrypted account data before the car self-locks. This is a time-limited traversal puzzle.
4. Storm Drain Exit: Smuggle witnesses through maintenance roads and underpasses while surface patrols tighten. This mission changes the play space.
5. Rain Of Receipts: Crash a debt auction, eliminate the marked bosses, and burn the claim archive before private reinforcements seal the exits. This closes Act II with direct class-war energy.

## Act III - Expose The Machine

### Chapter 13 - Civic Shield

Story role: Rook identifies the private security contractor that acts as the Switchboard's muscle.

Mission shape:

1. Training Day: Infiltrate a security proving route in a stolen cruiser and learn their patrol logic by staying inside formation. This is a disguise-and-discipline mission.
2. Panic Demo: Prevent a staged mall panic by disabling planted triggers before civilians stampede into kill zones. This is a proactive defusal mission.
3. Armor Column: Split an armored convoy by manipulating traffic lights and blockers instead of winning a straight firefight. This uses the city as a tactical tool.
4. Contract Burn: Enter the contractor archive and steal the live roster showing who is paid to arrive late, early, or never. This is an objective raid with heavy narrative value.
5. Black Badge Mile: Escape a district where every checkpoint has your photo and every cruiser has shoot-on-sight orders. The climax is endurance and routing, not a boss duel.

### Chapter 14 - The Missing Shift

Story role: Nia's last known allies were municipal dispatchers who vanished on the same night.

Mission shape:

1. Clock-In Ghosts: Visit empty apartments of three dispatchers and gather hidden clues before cleaners torch them. This is an investigative scavenger mission under time pressure.
2. Switch Room Bait: Restore power to a disused dispatch room, wait for whoever comes to shut it down, and capture them. This is a trap-and-hold mission.
3. Voiceprint Chase: Follow a spoofed radio caller by triangulating which district repeats the code phrase, then ram their escape route closed. This turns signal tracing into movement play.
4. Union Tunnel: Lead rescued dispatchers through a maintenance tunnel while enemies attack from both ends. This is a corridor survival escort.
5. Shift Ledger: Break into the central roster office and uncover that Nia was moved under a false death certificate. This chapter turns the search into a rescue mission.

### Chapter 15 - Low Water Mark

Story role: The city utility board is complicit, using flood gates and outages to steer panic.

Mission shape:

1. Valve Street: Reach four flood-control points before enemy crews open them fully. This is a route optimization mission under a soft fail state.
2. Cable Snare: Drag down surveillance cables with a heavy truck while under fire from rooftop marksmen. This mission uses vehicle mass as the main verb.
3. Dry Route: Escort evacuees through rising street water and dead vehicles, making navigation itself the enemy. This is a terrain hazard mission.
4. Pump House Red: Hold a pump station while mechanics restart it in stages, with each stage opening a new lane of attack. This is wave defense with spatial evolution.
5. Breakwater File: Drive a utility foreman out of the district alive after forcing them to reveal where Nia is being held. The payoff is information extracted through pressure, not murder.

### Chapter 16 - The Garden District Lie

Story role: Wealthy neighborhoods are receiving diverted emergency cover bought with the suffering of outer districts.

Mission shape:

1. White Curb Tour: Pose as valet transport to enter gated blocks and mark the hidden panic bunkers. This is social stealth through driving roleplay.
2. Garden Party Crash: Interrupt a donor gala by cutting arrival routes and forcing the guests into the street where they can be identified. This is a disruption mission.
3. Silver Plate Run: Steal a caterer's van loaded with guest records and keep the disguise long enough to cross the district. This rewards not attracting damage.
4. Iron Gate: Open three synchronized estate gates to let protest convoys in, then hold the corridors while the media gets the footage. This is coordinated map control.
5. Glass Lawns: Chase the district patron through ornamental roads, ponds, and dead-end gardens until they crash near a hidden bunker entrance. This closes the chapter with a stylized hunt.

### Chapter 17 - Blue Light Auction

Story role: Rook discovers that police response priority is literally being sold in secret meetings.

Mission shape:

1. Bid Card: Steal an invitation from a finance courier and arrive at the auction without raising heat. This is a quiet setup mission.
2. Table Service: Work the room disguised as staff, overhear the lot order, and tag the buyers' escape cars before the deal concludes. This mission is information-first.
3. Closing Bell: Trigger a fake bomb scare to empty the venue, then capture the auctioneer and their ledger. This is controlled chaos instead of a direct raid.
4. Car Park Scramble: Chase five buyers through an underground exit maze and decide which two matter most before the others vanish. This adds prioritization under pressure.
5. Priority Zero: Broadcast the auction ledger while surviving a coordinated police shutdown of the district. The player feels the machine resisting exposure in real time.

### Chapter 18 - Bone White Morning

Story role: Rook finally locates the site where missing witnesses and dispatchers were moved.

Mission shape:

1. Quarry Wake: Survey a remote industrial yard and photograph the routine without triggering full pursuit. This is a recon mission with fragile stealth.
2. Freight Coffin: Intercept a sealed transport truck, keep it intact, and discover live captives inside. This shifts the chapter from suspicion to horror.
3. Split Convoy: Separate decoy transports from the real one by using collisions, blockades, and stolen patrol authority. This is a thinking chase, not a straight race.
4. The Lower Bays: Enter the detention compound, rescue survivors cell by cell, and choose which route gets the bulk of them out. This is evacuation triage.
5. Nia's Voice: Recover Nia's recorded message explaining the Board and naming its four leaders, but learn she is still alive and being moved again. Act III ends with the personal stakes sharpened.

## Act IV - Break The Four Pillars

### Chapter 19 - The Broker

Story role: First pillar. The logistics broker controls freight rerouting and wreck monetization.

Mission shape:

1. Invoice Run: Hijack billing vans carrying destruction claims and deliver them to union auditors. This is document theft under pursuit.
2. Empty Chassis: Track a convoy of stripped cars and identify which shell hides the broker's private archive. This is a spot-the-pattern mission.
3. Yard Fire Drill: Trigger false alarms at three scrapyards to stretch the broker's crews thin before the real strike. This is preparatory sabotage.
4. Counterweight: Use heavy equipment to pin the broker's armored limo in a loading lane, then fight through the security response. This is a kinetic ambush.
5. Ledgers In The Furnace: Burn the live accounting vault while escaping through an active processing plant. The pillar falls through economic exposure and direct destruction.

### Chapter 20 - The Commissioner

Story role: Second pillar. The police commissioner sells selective law.

Mission shape:

1. Honor Guard: Blend into a ceremonial motorcade, stay in formation, and reach the commissioner's protected route. This is disguise under scrutiny.
2. Patrol Leak: Plant evidence into three command cars so rank-and-file officers see the corruption for themselves. This uses internal collapse instead of brute force.
3. Holding Pattern: Keep the commissioner trapped inside a traffic box you create with stolen buses and wrecks. This is spatial control as offense.
4. Blue Divide: Escort defecting officers out of the precinct while loyalists try to seal each junction. This mission is about faction fracture.
5. Open Channel: Force the commissioner to confess over police radio while surviving the last loyal response teams. The pillar falls through humiliation and proof.

### Chapter 21 - The Developer

Story role: Third pillar. The property developer engineers blight, fire, and collapse to flip whole districts.

Mission shape:

1. Model Unit: Infiltrate a show home to steal district redevelopment maps hidden in the sales office. This is a compact infiltration mission.
2. Controlled Demolition: Stop explosives from leveling an occupied block by cutting the sequence lines in the right order. This is a bomb-disarm variant.
3. Dust Run: Race a school bus out of a collapse corridor while debris closes alternate roads. This is a civilian rescue set piece.
4. Permit Office: Steal demolition approvals from a convoy and deliver them to the neighborhood coalition before they are digitally voided. This is a legal proof chase.
5. Foundation Crack: Bring down the developer's flagship tower by sabotaging support generators and escape before the panic crushes the plaza. The pillar falls through spectacle.

### Chapter 22 - The Minister Of Care

Story role: Fourth pillar. The health-services director weaponizes ambulance scarcity and body counts.

Mission shape:

1. Short Supply: Recover stolen medicine caches from fake clinics before the stock is resold. This is a reclaim mission with several small encounters.
2. Bed Count: Transport a whistleblower doctor across two active hot zones while preserving both passenger health and vehicle integrity. This is a combined escort and condition mission.
3. Intake Refusal: Force open a locked emergency entrance and keep it open long enough for civilians to flood in. This is breach-and-hold.
4. White Hallway: Move through a hospital annex where private guards and frightened staff mix together, making target identification part of the challenge. This changes combat readability.
5. Name The Dead: Upload the hidden casualty records from the morgue archive while the facility goes into total lockdown. The pillar falls through truth that cannot be buried.

### Chapter 23 - City Without Permission

Story role: The Board is wounded but responds by trying to shut the city down all at once.

Mission shape:

1. Grid Slip: Reconnect three severed neighborhoods using stolen utility trucks and emergency lane barriers. This is a support mission at city scale.
2. No Green Lights: Cross the entire map during a synchronized signal blackout while hostile drivers exploit the chaos. This is one long traversal ordeal.
3. Human Chain: Move supplies through a community convoy that cannot outfight the enemy and must instead outmaneuver them. This is cooperative convoy defense.
4. The Empty Broadcast: Reach the central tower and discover the Board has seeded a fake confession to discredit the resistance. This is a narrative reversal mission.
5. Hold Until Morning: Defend the tower overnight while allied factions coordinate the real counterstrike. This chapter is the final long-form survival mission before the finale.

### Chapter 24 - Dawn Through Concrete

Story role: Final chapter. Rook rescues Nia, destroys the Switchboard core, and decides what kind of city survives.

Mission shape:

1. Breach Window: Reach the hidden Switchboard facility through a moving sequence of access routes opened by allies. This is a culmination of every ally earned so far.
2. Live Wire: Escort Nia through the server plant while she remotely unlocks blast doors and enemies adapt to each reroute. This is a high-stakes moving escort.
3. Failsafe Fleet: Stop three destruction convoys heading toward the core's backup sites, forcing the player to prioritize route order and attack style. This is a multi-priority strike mission.
4. Heartbeat Zero: Hold the core room while Nia uploads the evidence and the Board tries to kill power, flood the chamber, and seal the exits. This is a final defense mission with layered hazards.
5. First Light Exit: Escape the collapsing facility, choose whether to release the raw data immediately or route it through trusted allies, and drive into a city that will survive but not stay the same. This ending leaves room for post-story free play and future content.

## Mission Variety Rules

To avoid repetition across 120+ story missions, the campaign set should deliberately rotate its pressures.

Rotate by pressure type:

- Time pressure: hard clocks, soft clocks, staggered deadlines, pursuit catch-up.
- Damage pressure: fragile vehicle, fragile cargo, fragile passenger, preserve disguise.
- Route pressure: checkpoints, closed roads, rising floodwater, blackout intersections, chokepoints.
- Detection pressure: tailing, low-profile driving, staying inside disguise rules, not killing civilians.
- Territory pressure: defend a corridor, hold a building, keep a route open, escort groups through crossfire.
- Prioritization pressure: choose which target to save, which convoy to chase first, which witness matters most.

Rotate by mission fantasy:

- Courier run
- Interception
- Tail and identify
- Rescue and extraction
- Siege defense
- Convoy attack
- Convoy protection
- Disguise infiltration
- Multi-stop setup
- Vehicle theft with constraints
- Sabotage and timed escape
- Rescue under live city hazards
- District-scale traversal ordeal
- Broadcast or upload holdout

Rotate by ending feel:

- Clean getaway
- Last-second delivery
- Survive until reinforcements
- Public reveal
- Target capture
- Structural destruction
- District liberation

## Design Notes For Future Campaign Conversion

When these chapters are converted into campaigns, each mission should include:

- One-sentence narrative hook.
- One primary success condition.
- One secondary pressure that changes how the player solves it.
- One clear failure state.
- One payoff that visibly changes the next mission.

Recommended campaign pacing:

- Mission 1 should teach the chapter's new twist.
- Mission 2 should complicate it.
- Mission 3 should flip the verb set.
- Mission 4 should punish complacency.
- Mission 5 should cash out the chapter and open a new story question.

## Grounded Implementation Plan

The older phase list is no longer a reliable execution order. It described a clean waterfall, but the prototype was built as a vertical slice, so story data, progression, UI, runtime actors, and tests were advanced in parallel to prove the mode. That was useful for discovery, but it is not the right plan for finishing the system safely.

From this point on, implementation should be base-first and gated. Several areas can still be researched in parallel, but code should only move forward when the lower layer is stable, covered by tests, and good enough to support the next layer without rework.

Execution rules:

1. Do not author broad new story content to compensate for weak runtime systems.
2. Do not add presentation complexity before the underlying runtime facts are stable and observable.
3. Every new base capability must land with unit or world coverage before it becomes a dependency for later story work.
4. Vertical slices are still useful, but only after the next required lower layer is complete enough to trust.

### Stage 3 - Finish Story Progression And Recovery (complete)

Goal: ensure long-form story runs are reliable before broadening content.

Why before presentation polish:

3. **Post-completion regressions found and fixed (user-reported)**:

1. Chapter-complete transitions were resetting the run instead of continuing it: the shared `pendingStoryRestart` handler in `CityScene` always cleared saved state and forced `skipResume:true` regardless of whether the restart was a chapter-complete advance or a mission-failure retry, so every chapter transition snapped the player back to the start position with money, ammo, and health reset. Fixed with a `pendingStoryRestartResume` flag, true only for chapter-complete advances, that skips the wipe and resumes from the just-persisted autosave instead (mission-failure retries still wipe, unchanged). That fix introduced its own follow-up regression: resuming via `World.fromSnapshot` also restored the just-finished (already-complete) chapter's `campaign`/`objectiveBaseline` from the snapshot, so the new chapter's first mission never actually loaded even though `storyProgress` bookkeeping had already advanced correctly — caught by running the full Playwright suite, not the new targeted test alone. Fixed with `World.resetActiveMission()` plus a `freshMissionOnResume` flag that rebuilds the campaign fresh from the new chapter's missions immediately after the snapshot restore; real save-slot loads and plain page-refresh resumes are untouched.
2. The manual-save "Load" button was a silent no-op: `storyLaunchState.ts`'s `isStoryProgressSnapshot` validator rejected `undefined` (only accepted `null`), and the Load button's launch request omits `storyProgress` entirely, so the whole launch request — including the target save slot — was discarded after the sessionStorage round-trip, and the scene silently fell back to the autosave every time instead of the selected slot. Fixed by accepting `undefined` in the validator.

Both were only caught by driving the real UI flow in new Playwright tests (clicking the actual Load button; letting the real chapter-complete restart timer fire) — the existing tests called scene/store APIs directly via `page.evaluate`, which bypassed the buggy code paths entirely.

Required tests before moving on:

2. Save/load and replay tests. — covered by `storyProgress.test.ts`'s "story progress persistence" suite (round-trip, malformed-save, migration) and `save-state.spec.ts`/`story-mode.spec.ts` (autosave-after-refresh, manual save/load slots, independent slots, chapter replay from the launcher, and "clicking the real Load-slot button in the story menu restores that slot" — the real-UI regression test for the Load-button fix above).
